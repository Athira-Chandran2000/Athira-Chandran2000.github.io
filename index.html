Research & Projects

Cross-Lingual Speaker Verification for Indic Languages

Developed a robust cross-lingual speaker verification system by pre-training on high-resource English (LibriSpeech) and fine-tuning on low-resource Indic languages such as Tamil and Malayalam. Implemented an advanced ECAPA-Conformer architecture with a multi-head attention mechanism to fuse spectral features (Log-Mel, MFCC) and prosodic features (pitch, energy). Enhanced embedding discriminability using a hybrid AAM-Softmax and Triplet loss, combined with extensive data augmentation techniques including SpecAugment, noise injection, and reverberation to improve robustness.

Multilingual Hate Speech Detection Using ML and Transformers

Developed a multilingual hate speech detection system for English and Malayalam social media datasets. Implemented and compared traditional machine learning models (Decision Tree, Random Forest, XGBoost), CNN-BiLSTM hybrid architectures, and transformer-based models including BERT, MuRIL, mBERT, and IndicBERT. Addressed class imbalance and optimized hyperparameters to ensure reliable performance on low-resource language data.

Indian Food Detection and Nutrient Estimation using YOLOv5

Built a computer vision-based data science pipeline to detect Indian food items and associate them with nutritional data. Compared multiple optimization strategies and achieved a mAP@0.5 score of 0.76. Reduced false positives by 18% through optimizer and confidence threshold tuning. Evaluated model behavior to understand limitations and real-world applicability.

Integrated Segmentation and Classification Framework for Neonatal HIE

Designed a two-stage framework combining deep learning-based medical image segmentation with machine learning classification to analyze neonatal HIE. Performed extensive data exploration and feature extraction to capture imaging patterns associated with disease severity. Achieved a Dice score of 0.749 and classification accuracy of 89%, with improved consistency across severity grades reflected by a macro F1-score of 0.83. Presented the work at IEEE INDICON 2025.
